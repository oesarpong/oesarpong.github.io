Lab 9: Mapping

Introduction

The goal of this lab is to map out a static room, which will later be used for localization and navigation tasks. This map will be created by placing the robot in several known locations around the room and incrementally rotate while collecting ToF readings. Assuming all goes well, the result in a map of the following setup.

Methodology: Orientation Control

To accomplish this goal of mapping, I decided to use a PID Orientational Controller to have the robot make 20 degree turns on-axis for a total of 18 setpoints. This alorithm will command the robot to attain its pose in accordance to the setpoint only logging data after reaching it. The returned measurement is an average of about 50 distance readings. The command is called once and iterates over a full 360 degree turn. Below is a video deomstrating the robots on-axis rotation.

Strategic Planning: Two ToF Sensors

My physcial setup contains two ToF sensors which are placed in parallel on opposite ends of the car and facing in opposite directions (see Lab 4 ). With this in mind, there are several ways in which this can be used to my advantage.

1) Faster Mapping: Instead of rotating the full 360 degrees, I can record the full scope with only a 180 degree turn

2) Averaged Results: Will complete a 360 degree turn and use both sensor data to improve the accuracy of my model with an averaged estimate

Both of these options prove to be useful in different scenarios. For this lab, I decided to use Option 2.

Discussion of Results/Issues

Overall, I am very pelased with the results. Although, there were significant challenges in completely this lab. I was running into a lot of EMI which was causing skewness in my data and at other times resulting in my ToF sensors freezing up. The EMI was a result of attempting to continously log ToF data which caused interferance between the 3 sensors running simultaneously. Additionally, my robot was moving out of place a lot due to repeated oscillations around the setpoint which would shift it backward and off center.

Success in this lab was found after simplifying my controller an seperating some of the actions taken by my robot through the mapping stage. The few discrepencies left were found when visualizing the results and were corrected during post processing.
